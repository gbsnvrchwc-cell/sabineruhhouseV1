Sabine Ruh House is an award-winning author whose work spans children's literature, poetry, and advocacy. A proud alumna of the University of Chicago, she brings the rigor of a medical researcher and the sensitivity of a lifelong poet to every page she writes.

Her books have been praised by Kirkus Reviews and embraced by educators, parents, and readers around the world. Whether exploring the tender territory of childhood mental health in Uncle Jay Has a Boo-Boo, the luminous world of synesthesia in Playing with Phosphenes, or the call to conservation in Wizardry Within, Sabine writes with a rare combination of scientific precision and lyrical grace.

Her work is shaped by a profound passion for medicine, gardening, animals, and advocacy — and by an unwavering belief that books can change how we see ourselves and each other.
